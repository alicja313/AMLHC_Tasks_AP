The data shows 17 different features and 2111 samples. Both include numerical and categorical variables. Numerical variables include: age, height, weight, FCVC (frequency of consumption of vegetables), NCP ( Number of main meals), CH2O (daily water intake), FAF (physical activity frequency). Model 2 which includes Age, Gender, family_history_with_overweight, and FAF in addition to SMOKE, appears to be a better model for predicting NObeyesdad compared to Model 1 (SMOKE alone). This conclusion is based on lower deviance values (both null and residual) and a lower AIC in Model 2 (1224.4), indicating better fit and predictive power. Therefore Model 2 should be preferred due to its better fit and inclusion of potentially important predictors (Age, Gender, family_history_with_overweight, and FAF). Also the p-value is 0.0651 which indicates that the association is not statistically significant. The intercept value (1.89120) is significant, as indicated by the p-value (< 2e-16) which means that there is still a baseline level of obesity when the person is not smoking. The answer is that smoking itself does not cause obesity moreover it is a multifactorial disease which is proven by model 2.

Interpretation:

The accuracy indicates that 96.19% are correct predictions. The CI (Confidence Interval) ranges from 0.9389 and 0.9781 which enough to state that these results can be achieved in the future again. The no information rate is 16.67% which means that the model is correct 16.67% times when just predicting the majority class. The p-value is very small which confirms that the model is significantly better. Kappa is a parameter for measuring the predictions and the actual outcome, a value of 0.9555 confirms that the results are accurate despite the possibility that they resulted by chance.

The different classes show three obesity types and three obesity levels as well as insufficient weight and normal weight. Overall a high Sensivity and Specifity were achieved (range 94.44% - 100%) . High Sensivity indicates that actual positives were correctly identified (less fals negatives) and high Specifity means that actual negatives were correctly identified (less false positives). The high Positive Predictive Value states that the values which were predicted to be positive were usually correct. Also the negative ones were correctly predicted. The detection rate has hardly any difference with the detection prevalence which proves that the proportion of predicted positives are correctly identified in each class.
